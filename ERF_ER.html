HBAI for Employers

Monthly (ER) Report Forms

Vitals

  • ER forms are used to report members hours and submit fringe benefit payment for a single agreement and work month.
  • Reports postmarked after the 25th day of the month following the work month will be considered delinquent.

Introduction

The paper form form is titled "Hawaii Carpenters Joint Trust Funds Employers Monthly Report To Trustees", which is quite a mouthful so we refer to it as the Monthly Report or the ER report. The ER is symbolic for Employ(ER) as opposed to Employ(EE) contributions. Spreadsheet forms are provided as a courtesy to simplify the fringe benefits payment process.

Here are some best practices and Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) regarding the Monthly Report. After that section are the downloadble report forms. Other topics may be covered in the Account FAQ or the Weekly Report FAQ.

Best Practices

Using The Spreadsheet Form

Here are some tips on using the Monthly Report spreadsheet forms to report fringe benefits payment for members. The forms also have instructions on the non-printing area of the Summary tab.

1. Make sure to fill in your employer information (red circles in the picture). The bank needs the information to accurately do your deposit. The State License Number field (orange circle in the picture), is optional. For this we are looking for your contractor license number if applicable, If you fill it in with something other than your account number, we will use it on any Trust Fund Clearance we issue to you. Sometimes the two can be completely different.
2. There are areas that use a drop-down list box. If you are unfamiliar with them you will click on the drop-down button (the red circle in the picture) which will cause a list of choices to appear (the green frame in the picture). Unfortunately there is no way to make the list items or the button any bigger, sorry.
3.

Areas of the spreadsheet are locked to protect the formulas and the ranges they depend on. It also prevents formulas from being overwritten accidentaly. You may want to let us the issue you are having rather than making changes to any locked cell ranges. However, if you must alter the form there is no password protection:

  • Select the cells that need to be unlocked.
  • Right click on the tab of the worksheet.
  • Select Protect Sheet on the context menu
  • on the Protect Sheet menu uncheck 'Protect worksheet and contents of locked cells' and click the OK button.
  • We suggest you lock the worksheet again after you are done.
4.

The adjustments line should be reserved for showing rounding differences or using Fund Credits Please do Apprentice Adjustments on a separate report.

5.

You may be able to use your own payroll reports in lieu of filling in the hours for each member. You will still need to fill in the summary page

  • We need approve the format of your payroll report before hand.
  • You will need to request the _g layout formo
    • OR
  • Use the _v layout as shown in the picture.

Apprentice Adjustments

The step-up notification for an apprentice always comes after they have moved up in classification, which means retroactive fringe benefits are due to them. The spreadsheet forms we provide can handily calculate these adjustment amounts due by fund. document the payment as well. Instructions are in the table below.

1. Change report class to 'Apprentice Adjustment'. Click on the drop-down list button (the red circle in the picture) and a list of choices will appear. Click on Apprentice Adjustments (the red arrow in the picture).
2. Doing the following will let the form do all the needed calculations for you. this method credits the previously paid fringes from the step-up class fringes over the same period.
  • List the same the member twice, once at the previous Apprentice Level and another time at the current Apprentice Level (the green frame in the picture).
  • In the previous Apprentice Level enter the retro hours as a negative number. For the current Apprentice Level enter the retro hours as a positive number (the red arrows in the picture).
3. That's it. Add more members and follow the same steps, or create a separate report for each member needing an apprentice adjustment. Here is what the computation table might look like for a single member.

FAQ

Spreadsheet Form Layouts

Each spreadsheet form layout was created to accomodate employers based on their roster size and payroll system functions. The table below will help determine which layout should suite you best. The spreadsheet forms overhaul in 2021 was also done to improve the accuracy of the totals on the summary worksheet.

Layout
Codes		 Name Usage
_v Vertical For reporting up to 17 members. Just a single page to print and remit.
_u Universal For employers whose payroll system report is not segregated by apprentice classification. A single tab to input up to 553 members and their apprentice class.
_s Standard For employers whose payroll system report is segregated by apprentice classification. Mimics the legacy form where each apprentice class has their own tab.
_g Generic Hours are summarized by apprentice class and input directly on the summary tab. Reserved for larger employers that will submit their own payroll system generated hours breakdown. Before receiving this spreadsheet your payroll report must be approved by HBAI.
_c Custom We can take your own payroll system apprentice codes and modify a spreadsheet to accept them. This would facilitate a direct copy and paste of your report with minimal editing. Can use use hourly wage instead of apprentice class for benefits calculation.
